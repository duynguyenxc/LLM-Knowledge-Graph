### Programme Theory: Enhancing Clinical Reasoning in Novice Learners

**What works, for whom, in what respects, to what extent, in what contexts, and how?**

#### Context (C)
The primary context involves novice learners, such as medical students, who possess 'low knowledge,' particularly in clinical domain-specific areas, and exhibit an inability to effectively use knowledge in reasoning situations. These students typically have limited clinical experience, which can impede their development of clinical reasoning skills.

#### Intervention
The intervention involves the use of teaching resources that allow students to make mistakes in a controlled environment. This approach is designed to facilitate the development of clinical reasoning abilities by providing a safe space for learners to explore and learn from their errors.

#### Mechanisms (M)
1. **Mechanism Resource (M1):** The intervention provides multiple relevant resources, including demonstrations of clinical consultations by experienced clinicians. These demonstrations occur at normal speed, simulating regular practice and showcasing a range of hypotheses and differential diagnoses considered during the consultation.
   
2. **Mechanism Response (M2):** Following the demonstration, the clinician deconstructs the consultation with the student. This deconstruction process is crucial as it assists the student in understanding the clinician's thought process and reasoning strategies. By breaking down the consultation, students can gain insights into the application of clinical knowledge and reasoning.

#### Outcome (O)
The outcome of this intervention is a positive impact on learning, specifically the encouragement of early development of clinical reasoning skills. By engaging with the resources and participating in the deconstruction process, students build a deeper understanding of clinical reasoning, which is essential for their future practice.

#### How it Works
In this educational framework, novice learners are placed in a context where they can observe and engage with clinical reasoning processes in a structured manner. The teaching resources, particularly the clinician-led demonstrations and subsequent deconstruction, serve as a catalyst for learning. By witnessing real-time clinical decision-making and then dissecting these decisions, students are able to bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application. This approach not only enhances their understanding but also builds their confidence in clinical reasoning, preparing them for more advanced clinical challenges.

#### Extent and Impact
The extent to which this programme theory is effective depends on the consistent application of the intervention and the active participation of both the clinician and the student. The positive impact on learning is most pronounced when students are given ample opportunities to engage with the resources and reflect on the deconstruction process. Over time, this method can significantly enhance the clinical reasoning capabilities of novice learners, equipping them with the skills necessary for effective medical practice.

In summary, this programme theory outlines a structured approach to developing clinical reasoning skills in novice learners by leveraging teaching resources that simulate real-world clinical scenarios and facilitate reflective learning.
